GORAKHPUR

Gorakhpur is a city situated along the banks of the Rapti River in the northeastern part of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It has a population of 673,446. Positioned near the Nepal border, Gorakhpur is 273 kilometres east of the state capital, Lucknow. Serving as the administrative headquarters of Gorakhpur district and Gorakhpur division, the city is renowned for the Gorakhnath Math, a temple dedicated to the medieval saint Gorakshanath. Gorakhpur holds significance as a religious center with historical ties to Buddhism, Hinduism, Jainism, and Sikhism. It derived its name from the medieval saint Gorakshanath, and the Gorakhnath Temple remains the seat of the Nath sect. The city boasts numerous historic Buddhist sites and is home to the Gita Press, a prominent publisher of Hindu religious texts. In the 20th century, Gorakhpur played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement. Today, it is not only a religious and historical hub but also a thriving business center. It houses the headquarters of the North Eastern Railways (www.nerailway.gov.in) and features an industrial area, GDA (Gorakhpur Development Authority), located 15 km from the old town.

CLIMATE

Gorakhpur experiences a pleasant environment throughout the year. In summer, the average temperature can rise as high as 40°C, while during the winter, it can drop as low as 4°C. The best time to visit Gorakhpur is during the months of October to March.

NEARBY TOURIST PLACES FOR VISIT

Gorakhnath Temple

Gorakhpur takes its name from Gorakhnath, who was a saint and traveled widely across India, authoring a number of texts that form a part of the canon of Nath Sampradaya. The Gorakhnath Math is a temple of the Nath monastic group within the Nath tradition, founded by Guru Matsyendranath. This math is situated in Gorakhpur, Uttar Pradesh, within a large premises. The temple engages in various cultural and social activities and serves as the cultural hub of the city. A shrine called Gorakhnath Mandir was built in his honor at the location. The musical Light and Sound Show in the evening is a main attraction. The temple continues to perform various cultural and social activities, acting as the cultural hub of the city. Gorakhnath Temple is 4.5 km away from the Gorakhpur Railway Station. Local transport such as auto, OLA, and taxis are easily available from the railway station. Gorakhnath Temple is 11.4 km away from Madan Mohan Malaviya University Of Technology, Gorakhpur. Local transport, including auto, OLA, and taxis, is easily available from Gorakhnath Temple to reach the college. Kushinagar

Kushinagar is a town in the Kushinagar district of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is an important Buddhist pilgrimage site where Buddhists believe that Gautama Buddha attained Parinirvana after his death. It is an international Buddhist pilgrimage center, attracting followers of Buddhism, especially from Asian countries, who wish to visit this place at least once in their lifetime. The road distance between Gorakhpur and Kushinagar is 51 km. For more detailed information on the location of Madan Mohan Malaviya University Of Technology from Kushinagar, click here.

ODOP Terracotta

Terracotta is a unique and special kind of ceramic craft. The term is commonly used for sculptures made in earthenware, as well as various utilitarian products, including vessels (notably flower pots), water and wastewater pipes, roofing tiles, bricks, and surface embellishment in building construction. What sets it apart from other terracotta crafts is its emphasis on ornamentation, the use of natural colors/dyes, and experimentation with innovative shapes. The raw material used for this craft is a type of locally available soil, which imparts a natural color to the item. Approximately 200 households are engaged in this work.

How to reach MMMUT

By Railway

Gorakhpur railway station serves as a vital connection to major Indian cities and is the headquarters of North Eastern Railway. Direct trains link it to Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Lucknow, Kanpur, Varanasi, and more. Situated on the basin of rivers Rapti and Rohini, Gorakhpur City has a unique bowl-shaped geography, with the west guarded by the Rapti river and the east associated with the Sal Forest. The city, known for its academic and medical institutions, boasts a university, a medical college, engineering and pharmacy colleges, management institutions, and more. Gorakhpur is a transportation hub, offering road connectivity to various cities and an airport for domestic travel. Key distances include Lucknow (276 km), Varanasi (231 km), Allahabad (339 km), Agra (624 km), Delhi (783 km), and Mumbai (1690 km). Despite its size, Gorakhpur hosts an army cantonment and an airbase, contributing to its cosmopolitan character. The renowned Gita Press adds an intellectual and religious dimension to the city. For transportation, direct trains run from major Indian stations, buses connect within the state and to Nepal and Delhi, and flights operate to/from Lucknow, Varanasi, and Delhi, though reliability may vary. By Road

There are a number of buses that provide regular services to different cities, including Kushinagar (50 km), Varanasi (235 km), Lucknow (276 km), and Allahabad (339 km). Both state-owned and private buses are available, which you can take to reach your desired destination. Direct buses from Gorakhpur to Lucknow and other cities are easily available.

Transport Facilities in Gorakhpur

Taxicabs can also be hired for a fixed time duration as desired by the user. Cycle rickshaw, a three-wheeled human-powered cycle, is the major mode of transport in the city. The charges are nominal and can be bargained. Usually, a ride of 1-1.5 km would cost around 20 Rupees. City buses are available for some important routes in the city. Auto-rickshaws are easily available for traveling to some parts of the city.
